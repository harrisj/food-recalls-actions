New York Firm Recalls Stuffed Chicken Products Due To Misbranding and Undeclared Allergens

Berk Lombardo Packing Co.. Inc.
Active
Wed, 11/23/2011 - Current

WASHINGTON, Nov. 23, 2011 - Berk Lombardo, a Brooklyn, N.Y. establishment, is recalling approximately 1,080 pounds of stuffed chicken products because of misbranding and undeclared ingredients, including known allergens. The products may contain any of the following combinations of undeclared ingredients: eggs, milk, monosodium glutamate...

Impacted Products
Boxes containing four, 6-oz. frozen, vacuum-sealed packages of "Berk Lombardo CHICKEN BREAST STUFFED BROCCOLI & CHEESE" that bears the establishment number "P-19034" inside the USDA mark of inspection. Boxes containing four, 6-oz. frozen, vacuum-sealed packages of "Berk Lombardo CHICKEN BREAST with rib meat CORDON BLEU BREADED" that bears the establishment number "P-19034" inside the USDA mark of inspection. Boxes containing four, 6-oz. frozen, vacuum-sealed packages of "Berk Lombardo STUFFED CHICKEN BREAST KIEV" that bears the establishment number "P-19034" inside the USDA mark of inspection.
