California Firm Recalls Ready-To-Eat Chicken Pasta Salad Products Due To Possible Salmonella Contamination

Closed
Fri, 11/05/2010 - Mon, 06/04/2012
California

WASHINGTON, Nov. 5, 2010 - APPA Fine Foods, a Corona, Calif., establishment, is recalling approximately 7,325 pounds of ready-to-eat chicken pasta salad products because they contain cilantro that may be contaminated with Salmonella, the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS) announced...

Impacted Products
12 oz. packages of " Spicy THAI STYLE PASTA SALAD"
Quantity Recovered
2,012.25 pounds
